AI Collaboration Guidelines

Your AI assistant is your Lab Partner, not your ghostwriter. You must engage with the AI at least three times. Adapt the following prompts for your chosen experiment.

💡

Prompt 1: Optimizing Setup (Before)

"I am a Secondary 4 Physics student in Hong Kong. I have chosen to do [Insert Your Selected Experiment] at home. For my setup, I plan to use [Insert Your Materials]. Before I begin, what are some potential systematic and random errors that might occur with this specific setup? How can I refine my design to ensure a fair and highly accurate measurement? Please suggest 3 practical improvements."
📊

Prompt 2: Troubleshooting & Verification (During/After)

"I have conducted my experiment and gathered the following data: [Insert Your Raw Data]. My calculated value for [Insert Your Variable] is [Insert your calculated value]. Please do not calculate the final values for me. Instead, help me analyze the physics... [Add specific questions related to your experiment's mechanisms]."
🧠

Prompt 3: Scientific Critique & Fact-Checking (Before Report)

"In high school physics, our textbook equations often describe 'ideal' conditions, like complete thermal isolation or motion without air resistance. In my real-world home experiment, why did I get values that differ from these ideal expectations? Where does the 'lost' energy actually go, and what real-world factors did our classroom models ignore?"

Investigation Report Structure

Your final written report (2-3 pages) must follow this exact structure. Clarity and critical reflection are key.

  1. Title Page: Name, Class, Class No, Date, Title.
  2. Objective & Hypothesis: Testing goals and expectations.
  3. Apparatus & Setup Diagram: Include a real annotated photograph.
  4. Procedure: Brief steps in passive voice.
  5. Data & Results:
    • Clear table with units.
    • Sample calculations (e.g., E_p = mgh).
    • Graphs where applicable.
  6. AI Collaboration & Critical Reflection (MOST IMPORTANT):
    • List primary prompts sent.
    • Summary of helpful advice.
    • Fact-check: Detail errors/unrealistic advice the AI gave and how you corrected it using physics concepts.
  7. Conclusion: Alignment with theory and personal learnings.
